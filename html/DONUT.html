Deliciously Delicious Donut

Indulge in a delightful homemade donuts with this recipe! These soft and fluffy treats are perfect for breakfast, brunch, or any time you crave a sweet and satisfying snack. This recipe yields 12 donuts, ensuring there's plenty to share with your loved ones. The donuts are infused with warm flavors of cinnamon and nutmeg, creating a comforting aroma that fills your kitchen as they bake. The batter is made with simple ingredients like flour, sugar, eggs, milk, and a touch of melted butter for richness. Once baked to golden perfection, these donuts are ready to be adorned with your choice of delectable glazes. From a classic glaze that adds a glossy sweetness to a decadent chocolate glaze for the chocoholics, and a vibrant matcha glaze for the adventurous, there's a glaze to suit every taste. Customize your donuts further by adding your favorite toppings like colorful sprinkles, crunchy nuts, shredded coconut, or mini chocolate chips. These assorted donuts are sure to bring joy to any gathering and make a delightful addition to your recipe collection. Get ready to savor each bite of these mouthwatering treats and experience the pure bliss of homemade donuts!

12 0:20 0:15 Medium
Servings Prep Time Cook Time Difficulty

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the classic glaze:

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • 3-4 tablespoons whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

For the chocolate glaze:

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 3-4 tablespoons whole milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

Optional toppings:

  • Sprinkles
  • Chopped nuts
  • Shredded coconut
  • Mini chocolate chips

Directions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and grease a donut pan.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, salt, cinnamon, and nutmeg.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and stir until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
  5. Spoon the batter into a piping bag or a large ziplock bag with the corner snipped off.
  6. Pipe the batter evenly into the donut pan, filling each cavity about 3/4 full.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the donuts are golden brown and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  8. Remove the donuts from the oven and let them cool in the pan for a few minutes before transferring them to a wire rack to cool completely.

For the glazes:

  1. To make the classic glaze, whisk together powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract in a shallow bowl until smooth.
  2. For the chocolate glaze, whisk together powdered sugar, cocoa powder, milk, and vanilla extract in a shallow bowl until smooth.
  3. To make the matcha glaze, whisk together powdered sugar, matcha powder, milk, and vanilla extract in a shallow bowl until smooth.

Assembly:

  1. Dip the cooled donuts into the glazes, covering them completely. Allow the excess glaze to drip off.
  2. Place the glazed donuts on a wire rack and add your desired toppings, such as sprinkles, chopped nuts, shredded coconut, or mini chocolate chips.
  3. Let the glaze set for about 10 minutes before serving.
